Phoebe Hedaux is a talented and passionate dancer from Spalding, Lincolnshire. She began her journey at just 7 years old, training in Acro at Spalding School of Dance.

At the age of 8, she transitioned to Diamonds, where she expanded her repertoire to include Musical Theatre, Street, Contemporary, and Lyrical under the guidance of Abbie, a dedicated coach with a focus on mental health awareness.

In early 2023, at the age of 13, Phoebe joined Empire Elite All Stars, competing in Level 2 Cheerleading, Street, Pom, and Lyrical. By 2024, she advanced to Level 3 Cheerleading and played a crucial role in securing the Grand National Championship title in Bournemouth. In 2024, Phoebe was also accepted into Team Englandâ€™s Pom Development Squad, where she trains in Telford every six weeks, further honing her skills on a national level. Her dedication, versatility, and drive continue to push her towards new heights in the dance industry.

Throughout 2024-2025, she continued competing with Cheer City and Legacy, achieving victory at the Birmingham NEC in the Level 3 U16s Cheerleading division. Alongside her competitive cheerleading, Phoebe pursued further dance education with Elizabeth Boardman in Peterborough in early 2025. She is now focused on Modern dance, working towards graded levels while training in Senior Street, Senior Street Team (Dance Company), Grade 7 Modern, Grade 6 Ballet, Senior Tech (Dance Company), Senior Contemporary, and Senior Contemporary Team (Dance Company).
